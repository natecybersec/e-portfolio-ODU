  • Week 3: Describe four ethical issues that arise when storing electronic information about individuals.

  • Electronic information, according to the “Information Technology Resource Management Glossary”, is known as “any information stored in a format that enables it to be read, processed, manipulated, or transmitted by an information system.” Electronic information can be stored on devices like USB drives, hard drives, or even on a remote server. A variety of ethical issues involved in storing electronic information about individuals come to mind. First, an issue that is still very controversial to this day is that most of the public are not aware of what information about them is being stored, much less the specifics of said information. This means that individuals may not be able to give their consent to store information in an informative manner, and if the information becomes leaked, they may be surprised to learn how much unwanted information will be released to the internet. In addition to this, an individual’s information that they may want to keep private may be accessed by insiders of companies, most notably social media providers like TikTok and Facebook. These actions could result in a serious violation of one’s privacy rights here in the United States. Electronic information about an individual that is stored could also misrepresent an individual, as the information could be inaccurate, especially concerning the age of the data versus the individual’s current and up-to-date information. This gets especially complicated when considering that companies often partake in selling user data to advertising partners. Fortunately for the average user, there are ways to take control of their data and the specifics of what is stored by different companies or organizations. Users should always read the fine print when prompted with data collection, as they can learn more about how an organization stores user data and the contents of what they are storing. Individuals will find that some companies will actually let one opt out, thus negating ethical issues. If it’s the case that you can’t fully opt-out, even then a user should be able to opt out of the selling of their data to other organizations. The ethical issues that arise from storing electronic information about a user should be clear to all organizations who take part, and should be done in a manner that respects an individual’s rights.

  • Week 4: Compare cybersecurity risks in the U.S. and another country.

  • The risks that cybersecurity tries to mitigate cannot be understated. Critical infrastructure such as power grids or financial systems, sensitive user data collected by organizations, and computer systems in general are all targets of attackers. When discussing the risks to the United States’ critical infrastructure’s cybersecurity, there are numerous factors that can contribute. The U.S. is in a unique position due to it natural size, thus the infrastructure is vast and complicated, including an expansive variety of systems and machines that hold things together. Due to the size, it also makes it harder to defend, especially with the seperation of federal, state, and local government oversight. Different states might have different standards for how they protect their infrastructure, with some states leading and some falling behind due to negligent management. Compared to other countries, because of their smaller size, it’s most likely a lot easier to provide oversight to their critical infrastructure and provide a stronger defense. It also makes it a lot easier to update a smaller number of systems than it is a larger number, which results in better defense against zero-day exploits. In terms of risks to sensitive user data collected by organizations, both the U.S. and other countries have their fair share of issues to tackle, since said data is an important commodity globally. Social media is extremely prominent in the world today, with enormous amounts of data being passed back and forth all over the globe. Fortunately, the western part of the world has taken and are continuing to take steps toward protecting users and their right to privacy. The U.S. has various agencies and commissions, like the Federal Trade Commission, that produce safeguards for user data. The European Union has the “General Data Protection Regulation”, also known as “GDPR”, which pushes a set of requirements for organizations to protect user data, as well as notify users of data breaches in a timely manner.

  • Week 6: How has cyber technology created opportunities for workplace deviance?

  • The development of cyber technology has created a new form of workplace deviance. It encompasses the acts of misusing computers, networks, and other technological resources possibly for one’s own personal gain, or to commit a malicious act. This type of deviance can be perpetrated through a variety of avenues, including email, instant messaging, social media, and even through the use of CCTV cameras. One of the most common forms of cyber workplace deviance is data theft of confidential information, such as customer credit cards, corporate passwords, and price lists. This type of data theft can often occur when employees gain access to unauthorized networks or areas of data storage. Additionally, cyber technology can further accentuate workplace deviance through the use of the internet for inappropriate activities, such as playing games, accessing or downloading copyrighted software, or even sending or receiving emails that could harm the company’s reputation or business. In addition to data theft, cyber technology has enabled other types of workplace deviance, such as intellectual property theft, or the misuse of company computer systems for unauthorized activities, such as accessing adult websites. Cyber deviance can also extend to the physical world, with employees using cameras or recording devices to capture confidential meetings or private conversations. Furthermore, in the age of social media with employees whose jobs are solely dedicated to running social media accounts for an organization, there have been instances of employees using social media networks to post false information and communicate false threats on behalf of the company. The proliferation of new cyber technology has both enabled, and decreased the barriers to workplace deviance. On one hand, it has made it easier for employees to get away with stealing confidential data and engaging in other forms of deviant behavior due to the increased anonymity it provides. On the other hand, companies now have the ability to trace many of these activities through forensic investigation and online monitoring systems. This can provide valuable evidence when trying to identify and prosecute those responsible, while also generating awareness of the potential consequences of cyber workplace deviance.

  • Week 7: What are the costs and benefits of developing cybersecurity programs in business?

  • Developing cybersecurity programs in businesses can be a costly endeavor, but often times the benefits outweigh the costs. A comprehensive program and strategy should start with identifying risks and assessing the company's current cybersecurity infrastructure, which often requires professional investment. Developing a framework for data protection, as well as recruitment of cybersecurity personnel, can also involve hefty expenses. However, organizations benefit from mitigating cyber threats by protecting their information and investments, avoiding costly data breaches, and safeguarding their customers and their trust. Having a strategy for responding quickly to security events and subsequent investigations can help to limit the damage, protect sensitive data and build stakeholder confidence. This can also prevent legal and reputational costs, and even regulatory or governmental penalties. Technology investments can also play a key role in developing a cybersecurity program. Firewalls, anti-malware software, intrusion detection systems and encryption are necessary steps to secure data, protect communications, and prevent unauthorized access. Advanced security measures, such as multi-factor authentication and data loss prevention, can help to further protect information. Organizations may even invest in processes such as control access granting and user behavior analytics, to track abnormal activity in real-time, and give an early warning if something is amiss. Training initiatives may be integral to ensure a secure environment, and ensure employees are up-to-date on the latest security protocols within the organization. This can also improve relationships with customers, who will see that their data is being kept secure and private. Organizations should also have protocols to monitor, update and test their systems, in order to keep them running safely and efficiently. In conclusion, the costs of developing a comprehensive cybersecurity program in businesses can often be outweighed by the benefits, including increased data security, mitigated threats, improved customer confidence, and compliance with legislation. Investing in the right personnel, processes and technology can be the key to ensuring a secure environment.

  • Week 8: How can you tell if your computer is safe?

  • An individual can tell if their computer is safe by assessing certain indicators that could suggest the presence of malicious software or other security threats. To begin, it is important for the individual to ensure that their computer is running the latest version of their operating system and any other software running on the machine, as well as its anti-virus program. Newer versions of software often include bug fixes and security updates, which can help protect against malicious attacks. The individual should also pay attention to any new or unfamiliar pop-ups, messages, or browser toolbars that appear on their computer. These could be signs of malicious programs installed on the computer. They should also monitor their computer's performance and look for any sudden decreases in speed or lagging that could be a result of an infection. Individuals can also look at their browsing history to make sure they have not unwittingly visited malicious websites that could have installed malicious programs on their computer. Additionally, they should perform periodic system scans with their anti-virus to check for any malicious software or viruses. For further security, individuals can also use firewalls and VPNs to secure their connections and encrypt their data. This helps protect their personal information from being accessed by hackers or other malicious actors. Last but not least, individuals should make sure that their passwords are strong, and that they are not reusing passwords for multiple sites or services. If they take all the necessary steps, they should be able to tell if their computer is safe or not. However, they should also be aware that no security measures are 100% foolproof, and they should stay up-to-date with the latest security news to keep their system protected. They should also use caution when downloading files, and make sure they only download files from trusted sources. All of these steps should help protect their computer and help them decide on whether or not they are within a safe digital environment.

  • Week 9: Describe three ways that computers have made the world safer and less safe.

  • Computers have made the world safer by improving security systems with the help of an enhanced data storage system, which helps keep sensitive personal and financial data away from potential cyber criminals, as well as allowing for faster processing of security checks. For example, there are now banks that use biometric technology to authenticate legit customers, improving overall safety for all transactions. Furthermore, with the power of machine learning and predictive analytics, computers have been able to detect suspicious activities ahead of time, allowing for proactive steps to be taken to prevent any potential crime from taking place. Secondly, computers have significantly reduced workplace fatalities by automating dangerous and often tedious tasks. Many industrial tasks that had been extremely dangerous for workers can now be completed with the help of computers, which drastically reduces the risk of accidents. Computers can also be used to monitor the safety of industrial production plants, identifying possible hazards before they occur and alerting workers to take the necessary precautions. Thirdly, computers have also helped to further police and law enforcement's ability to apprehend criminals. With the help of sophisticated software and facial recognition technology, police can identify criminals more quickly and with greater accuracy. Additionally, computers have also allowed for smarter management of data, allowing police and law enforcement to store and organize files more efficiently, allowing for faster and more effective investigation processes. Computers have also made the world a more dangerous place as well. In recent years, computers have become more and more integrated into daily life, and thus ever more vulnerable to cyberattacks. Computers have made the world less safe by providing attackers with an ever-increasing attack surface. Digital criminals can gain access to sensitive data, steal valuable information, and sabotage computer networks with their malicious activities. With the rise of powerful programs for distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks, virtual extortion, and other scams, it’s more important than ever to stay ahead of hackers. Computers have made us more vulnerable to attack by introducing a whole new set of unsecured devices. Smartphones, tablets, and other Internet-connected gadgets bring a wide range of convenience and access to digital content, but they also open the door to attackers. Unsecured devices are potential points of entry for malware, allowing attackers to gain access to sensitive data. Thanks to computers, social engineering has become an increasingly prevalent security threat. Although attackers often use malicious software to gain access to people’s systems, there are multiple ways into a system. they craft clever emails to steal confidential information, and they use psychological manipulation to gain access to protected accounts. Online users, especially those working remotely or handling sensitive data, must maintain a vigilant defense against social engineering attacks. In all, computers have made the world less safe by increasing the attack surface and provides attackers with numerous opportunities to exploit our systems.

  • Week 10: How do engineers make cyber networks safer?

  • Engineers work continually to make cyber networks safer and more secure. They develop and implement a variety of technologies, processes and best practices to protect these networks and the data that resides within them. For example, engineers use encryption to encode data transmitted over networks, making it very difficult for attackers to glean any information. They also use authentication to ensure that only authorized users or systems can gain access to data and networks. Engineers monitor network activity to detect malicious activity and unauthorized attempts to access data, and then apply appropriate measures to stop or contain such activities. Firewalls are another tool used to help protect networks. Firewalls are essentially filters that are used to protect computers from malicious software entering via the Internet. Engineers use various techniques to configure firewalls so that only approved traffic can pass through. They also use antivirus software to scan for and remove malicious code that may have already infiltrated a system. Other steps engineers take to protect networks include developing secure coding practices, regularly patching or fixing software vulnerabilities, and restricting user privileges so that users cannot access data they are not authorized to access. They may also engage in penetration testing to detect potential vulnerabilities in networks and systems. In addition to installing and configuring various security tools and systems, engineers must also stay up to date on the latest threats and trends in the industry. They attend conferences and seminars, read industry publications, and actively participate in security research to ensure they have the latest techniques and approaches to protect networks. The job of an engineer is never done when it comes to ensuring the safety and security of cyber networks. To make sure cyber networks are safe and secure, engineers must continually monitor for threats, implement new security measures, and stay informed on the latest trends in the security industry.

Cybercrime is a term that is broadly used to describe criminal activity that involves the use of computers, networks, or other forms of technology to commit a criminal offense. This form of crime is growing significantly each year due to the fact that technology is used widely in our society and criminals are adapting to this new environment. This type of crime requires special attention from law enforcement and criminal justice professionals as the complexity of the crimes involved is typically higher than those found in the traditional world. The overlap between criminal justice and cybercrime can be seen in a variety of areas. One is the need for law enforcement and criminal justice professionals to become increasingly well-versed in computer technology and its associated networks and systems. This involves having a deep understanding of the technical architecture of a network, the availability of tools to access and manage networks, and the methods used by cybercriminals to gain unauthorized access. In addition, law enforcement and criminal justice professionals need to be prepared to respond to cyber-attacks and other forms of computer crime. This includes the ability to investigate and prosecute cybercrime cases, as well as to investigate and properly use digital evidence for court proceedings. In this area, there is an increased need for expertise in the fields of digital forensics and the law to effectively investigate the case and present a credible case in court. Finally, criminal justice professionals need to understand the legal and ethical implications of cybercrime in order to ensure that its victims receive justice. In this area, there is a need to understand the legal precedents that have been set in relation to cybercrime, as well as the development of specialized statutes and laws to respond to such crimes. These overlaps relate other disciplines due to the fact that cybersecurity remains a joint effort by multiple fields to make the internet a safer place. It requires legislature, law enforcement, engineers, and many more career paths to keep cybersecurity systems up to date.

  • Week 12: How does cyber technology impact interactions between offenders and victims?

  • Cyber technology has drastically changed the nature of interactions between victims and offenders, as it improves the ability to easily connect with people from all over the world, enabling criminals to target victims from locations other than their own. With advancements in technology, those committing criminal acts have a much wider scope of access with which to prey upon victims, this can in turn have a severe impact on the physical and mental wellbeing of the victim. Cyber criminals can commit all kinds of acts that they were unable to do before, such as identity theft, online scams, cyberstalking, cyberbullying, phishing, spam campaigns and other malicious acts. Some offenders may even use cyber technology to obtain personal information of their victims and craft a highly tailored attack. Victims can be exposed to sensitive material, blackmailed with threats to release said material, or coerced into sending money and/or sensitive information to the offender. This type of criminal activity can also have residual impacts on the victims, some of which can be long-lasting. Victims may have an increased risk of mental illness, suicidal thoughts, physical health issues, and social isolation due to the humiliation or embarrassment of the attack. This can be especially true for victims of cyberbullying and cyberstalking, who may experience prolonged distress due to the ongoing nature of the attack. To help combat cybercrimes, organizations such as law enforcement, financial institutions, and social media companies are developing processes and protocols to detect and respond to cyber threats, often in collaboration with government agencies dedicated to protecting citizens online. Non-profit organizations also create campaigns to spread awareness to the public about the dangers of internet activity. Despite these efforts, victims and potential victims should practice online safety by using strong passwords, being vigilant when sharing personal information, and reviewing account activity regularly on all devices.

  • Given the challenges associated with the "short arm" of predictive knowledge when developing cyber-policy and infrastructure, a multi-faceted approach is necessary to ensure that we are able to anticipate potential threats in an ever-changing technological landscape. First, we must actively monitor the cyber-landscape and employ a strategic response capability. This includes deploying innovative technologies and e-government platforms that can reduce the time required to detect and respond to cyber-attacks. It also means leveraging big data and analytics to better identify and anticipate potential threats, as well as providing adequate security budgeting for the development of effective policy and infrastructure. Second, a culture of cybersecurity awareness and education must be fostered to ensure that both public and private sector personnel are equipped to understand the implications of cyber-attacks. This includes engaging with the community to promote e-safety, teaching methods to detect and respond to security issues, and providing resources to ensure everyone understands the dangers of cyber-crime. Third, organisations must initiate a long-term commitment to continually review and update their cyber-policy and infrastructure. This means examining existing policies and protocols to ensure they remain up-to-date and are responsive to the needs of the ever-changing digital landscape. It also means equipping staff with the necessary skills to respond swiftly to potential threats. Finally, ensuring we have the resources and infrastructure necessary to quickly hear and respond to the cyber-world is critical. This includes building a secure digital environment with robust architecture and implementing best practice security processes for the protection of critical data. The cyber-world will continue to evolve at a rapid pace, therefore it is important to have an agile and effective approach to policy and infrastructure. By leveraging the latest technologies and analytical frameworks, as well as actively engaging with the community, organisations can ensure they are at the forefront of cyber-security and able to create a safe and secure digital environment.
